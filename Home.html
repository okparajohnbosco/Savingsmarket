What is a savings account?

A savings account is a type of bank account
where you put money that you don't need to
spend immediately. You deposit money with
the bank and get paid interest in return for
saving with them.

Savings accounts are simpler than current
accounts, which are designed for everyday
banking like paying bills. For example, you
can't have an overdraft with a savings account.
A savings account is there for you to put
money away safely so it's available when you
need it. It can help you build up a lump sum,
and earn you interest on money that you don't
need right now, for other things.
